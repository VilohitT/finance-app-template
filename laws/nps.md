## 1. NPS basics

- Defined-contribution retirement scheme regulated by PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority)
- Permanent Retirement Account Number (PRAN) — portable across employers and locations
- Two account types: **Tier 1** (lock-in to retirement, full tax benefits) and **Tier 2** (no lock-in, no tax benefits for private sector)
- Eligible: any Indian citizen aged 18-70 (NRIs eligible too)
- Asset allocation choice: **Active** (custom % across asset classes) or **Auto** (lifecycle fund — equity glides down with age)

---

## 2. Asset class options under NPS

Tier 1 contributions allocate across:
- **E (Equity):** capped at 75% in Active mode for ages up to 50; reduces gradually after
- **C (Corporate Bonds):** can be 100% if chosen
- **G (Government Securities):** can be 100% if chosen
- **A (Alternative Assets — REITs, INVITs, AIFs):** capped at 5%

Tier 2 has more flexible allocation but **no tax benefits for private sector employees**, so it's largely a flexible mutual-fund-like wrapper without compelling advantage.

## 3. Section 80CCD — the critical tax provisions

### 3.1 Section 80CCD(1) — Employee's own contribution
| Item | Old Regime | New Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Deduction limit | Salaried: 10% of salary (Basic+DA), capped at ₹1.5L within overall 80CCE | NOT AVAILABLE |
| Self-employed | 20% of gross income, capped at ₹1.5L | NOT AVAILABLE |
| Counts toward | Combined ₹1.5L 80C/80CCC/80CCD(1) limit (Section 80CCE) | — |

### 3.2 Section 80CCD(1B) — Additional ₹50K
| Item | Old Regime | New Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Deduction limit | Additional ₹50,000 above the ₹1.5L 80C cap | NOT AVAILABLE |
| Above and beyond | 80C / 80CCD(1) limit | — |

This is the most attractive "additional" deduction under Old Regime — exists only for NPS, doesn't compete with PPF/EPF/ELSS for cap space.

### 3.3 Section 80CCD(2) — Employer's contribution (the only NPS deduction available under New Regime)
| Item | Old Regime | New Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Govt employees | 14% of salary (Basic+DA) | 14% of salary (Basic+DA) |
| Private employees | 10% of salary (Basic+DA) | **14% of salary (Basic+DA)** — raised in Budget 2024, effective FY 2025-26 |
| Above and beyond | 80C and 80CCD(1)+1(B) limits | Standalone, not constrained by other ceilings |

Combined cap: employer NPS + EPF + superannuation contributions cannot exceed **₹7.5 lakh per FY** combined. Excess becomes taxable in the year of contribution.

### 3.4 Implications for the household decision

For user under **New Regime** (default):
- Personal NPS contributions: zero tax benefit. Effectively a long-term equity-debt wrapper with lock-in to 60 and no advantage over a direct MF holding.
- Employer NPS via 80CCD(2): can be very valuable. If employer restructures CTC to add a 14% Basic+DA NPS contribution, that amount is fully deductible without affecting any other limit. For user with ~₹40K basic, this is ~₹67K/year of tax-saved retirement saving — at no cost to user, just CTC re-allocation.
- **The single highest-leverage NPS action under New Regime is asking HR if the employer offers / can offer 80CCD(2) contribution.**

For user under **Old Regime** (if chosen):
- Personal NPS up to ₹1.5L → 80CCD(1) deduction (within 80C cap shared with PPF/EPF/ELSS — likely already filled)
- Personal NPS additional up to ₹50K → 80CCD(1B) deduction (standalone, very attractive)
- Employer NPS up to 10% Basic+DA → 80CCD(2) deduction (still over and above 80C limits)

For father (business owner, separate filing):
- 80CCD(1) — up to 20% of gross income, ₹1.5L cap
- 80CCD(1B) — additional ₹50K (Old Regime only)
- No 80CCD(2) — no employer
- However, **father's horizon (5-10 years to business wind-down, age 58 now) is too short for NPS Tier 1's lock-in to 60.** Even if eligible, the principle is clear: NPS is a user-only deployment in this household. (See `principles.md` Section 4.1 item 2.)

## 4. Lock-in and exit rules — Tier 1

### 4.1 Normal exit (at age 60)
- **60% of corpus tax-free** as lump-sum withdrawal (Section 10(12A))
- **40% of corpus must be used to purchase an annuity** (mandatory)
- Annuity income is taxable at slab rate in subsequent years
- Annuity is purchased from one of PFRDA-empanelled annuity service providers (LIC, HDFC Life, ICICI Prudential, etc.)

### 4.2 Premature exit (before age 60)
- Allowed but with constraints:
  - **80% must be used for annuity** (only 20% as lump sum)
  - Lump sum portion is tax-free; annuity income taxable at slab
- After 5 years of contribution: can also withdraw partially (up to 25% of own contributions) for specific purposes (illness, education, marriage, house purchase) — limited to 3 such withdrawals over the account's life

### 4.3 Death of subscriber
- Entire corpus paid to nominee/legal heir
- Tax-free in nominee's hands (Section 10(12A) covers the lump sum portion)
- Nominee can choose to take full corpus as lump sum or buy annuity

### 4.4 NPS Vatsalya (added 2024)
- Account for minor child, operated by parent/guardian
- Same 80CCD(1B) ₹50K deduction available for parental contributions (Old Regime)
- Becomes a regular NPS account when child turns 18

**For this household:** not currently relevant (kids excluded per goals.md). Becomes relevant post-first-child re-grill trigger.

## 5. Costs

NPS is among the lowest-cost market-linked retirement products in India:
- **Investment management fee:** 0.03% to 0.09% per annum (significantly below most MFs)
- PRA (Permanent Retirement Account) maintenance: ₹95/year
- Transaction charges: minor, varies by Point of Presence (POP)

Total expense ratio works out to ~0.10-0.15% per annum — about an order of magnitude lower than active equity MFs and below even most index funds.

## 6. Tier 2

- No lock-in
- Withdraw any time
- **Private sector: no tax benefits** (was deductible for govt employees in some past structures; current rules essentially make Tier 2 a regular investment wrapper)
- Tier 2 redemption: capital gains taxed similar to mutual funds (equity allocation determines treatment)
- Generally not recommended for this household — direct equity / debt MF holdings achieve the same flexibility without the NPS overhead

---

## 7. Operational mechanics

- Account opening: via NPS Trust website, eNPS portal, or any POP (most banks). Aadhaar-based eKYC supports paperless opening.
- Contributions: minimum ₹500 per contribution; minimum ₹1,000/year to keep account active
- Asset allocation can be changed up to 4 times per year
- Can switch between pension funds (HDFC Pension, ICICI Prudential Pension, SBI Pension, etc.) once per year
