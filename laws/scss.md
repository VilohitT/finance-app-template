## 1. Current interest rate

- **8.20% per annum** for FY 2025-26 (all four quarters confirmed at 8.20%, including Jan-Mar 2026 per Finance Ministry announcement dated 31 December 2025)
- Compounded **quarterly** and paid quarterly to the linked savings account on the 1st of April, July, October, January
- Rate is reviewed quarterly; among the highest-yielding fixed-income small savings schemes available

**As-of:** Q4 FY 2025-26 (Jan-Mar 2026) — 8.20% confirmed.

---

## 2. Eligibility

- **Indian residents aged 60 and above**
- Retired civilian employees aged **55 to under 60**, provided investment is made within **1 month** of receipt of retirement benefits
- Retired defence personnel aged **50 to under 60**, same 1-month rule
- HUFs are **not** eligible (rule changed; existing HUF SCSS accounts grandfathered from earlier era)
- NRIs are not eligible
- Joint account: only with **spouse**; investment is attributed to first holder

**For this household:**
- Father becomes eligible in 2028 at age 60. Standard route — no early retirement window applies based on the household profile.
- User is not eligible (would need to wait 35+ years).
- Mother becomes eligible at her own 60. If investing both, two SCSS accounts are possible — combined ₹60L capacity (₹30L each).

---

## 3. Contribution

- **Minimum: ₹1,000**, in multiples of ₹1,000
- **Maximum: ₹30,00,000** per individual (raised from ₹15L in Budget 2023)
- One-time deposit at account opening; cannot top up existing account incrementally for the same individual (must open fresh accounts subject to the ₹30L overall ceiling per person)
- Deposits up to ₹1,00,000 can be made in cash; above ₹1L by cheque/DD/online transfer

**Capacity for this household in 2028:** father can deposit up to ₹30L. If mother is also eligible at the same time, household capacity = ₹60L.

---

## 4. Tenure

- **5 years** initial maturity
- Extendable in **3-year blocks** (extension extends rate at the time of extension, not original opening)
- After extension: account can be closed any time after 1 year from extension date with no penalty
- Multiple extensions are allowed (3 years at a time)

---

## 5. Premature closure

- Allowed any time, with penalty schedule:
  - **Within 1 year:** no interest paid; if any interest credited, recovered from principal
  - **1 to under 2 years:** 1.5% of principal as penalty
  - **2 to under 5 years:** 1% of principal as penalty
- After 5-year maturity (or after extension): no penalty for closure

## 6. Tax treatment

### 6.1 Principal contribution
- **Section 80C deduction up to ₹1.5L** under **Old Regime only**
- No deduction under New Regime
- Counts toward overall 80C cap — competes with PPF/EPF/ELSS/insurance/etc.

### 6.2 Interest income
- **Fully taxable** in the hands of the depositor at slab rate
- Interest is paid out (not reinvested in the account), so it appears as taxable income each year
- Section 80TTB allows seniors a deduction of **up to ₹50,000** on interest income from deposits (savings, FDs, RDs, SCSS, post office time deposits combined). Same person cannot also claim 80TTA.

### 6.3 TDS
- TDS at 10% if total annual interest credited exceeds **₹1 lakh** (raised from ₹50,000 in Budget 2025)
- For depositors with overall income below the taxable threshold: submit **Form 15H** to avoid TDS at source

### 6.4 Net post-tax yield estimation for father (assuming 30% bracket)

If father is in 30% marginal tax bracket on the SCSS interest:
- Gross yield: 8.20%
- After 80TTB deduction (₹50K) and slab tax: depends on father's other income
- Worst case (full slab rate, no 80TTB): 8.20% × (1 - 0.312) = **~5.65% post-tax**
- Best case (interest fully covered by 80TTB): close to 8.20% net for the first ₹50K of interest

For a ₹30L SCSS deposit: annual interest = ₹2.46L. The first ₹50K is sheltered by 80TTB (under Old Regime); the remaining ₹1.96L is taxable. At 30% bracket: tax = ₹61K, post-tax interest = ₹1.85L on the ₹30L → effective yield **~6.17% net**.

**Comparison to alternatives for father at age 60:**
- SCSS net 6.17% (above)
- Tax-free bonds (if available): typically 5.5-6% gross = same net yield, but lock-in 10-15+ years
- RBI Floating Rate Bonds: rate floats, currently ~8.05% gross, slab-rate taxable → similar to SCSS
- Bank FDs (senior citizen): 7.5-8% gross at top banks, slab-rate taxable → slightly worse than SCSS
- Debt MFs: 6.5-7.5% gross, slab-rate taxable → significantly worse, plus principal volatility

**Conclusion:** SCSS is the strongest deployment for father's age-60 senior debt sleeve, particularly when paired with 80TTB. The principle is: roll over debt MF holdings into SCSS at father's 60, up to the ₹30L cap.

## 7. Operational mechanics

- Open at any nationalised bank, major private bank, or post office
- Required documents: PAN, Aadhaar, age proof (60+ or retirement proof if 55-60), passport-size photos, retirement benefit proof if applicable
- Nomination supported (must be done at opening or via subsequent nomination form)
- Quarterly interest payout cannot be reinvested into the same SCSS account; goes to linked savings account
- Multiple accounts allowed in the same individual's name as long as combined deposit ≤ ₹30L

## 9. Risks and watchpoints

- **Rate revision risk:** rate is set quarterly by Ministry of Finance. The rate at deposit applies for the 5-year tenure; subsequent rate cuts don't affect existing deposits, but extensions get the rate at extension time.
- **Liquidity:** SCSS is locked for 5 years with penalty for early closure. Father's debt allocation should not be 100% in SCSS — keep some in PPF and short-duration debt MFs for liquidity.
- **₹30L ceiling per person:** large household debt allocations may exceed combined SCSS capacity (₹60L if both parents eligible). Excess flows to RBI FRB / debt MFs.
- **Quarter-end deposit timing:** interest accrual starts from deposit date but is paid only on quarter-ends (1st of Apr/Jul/Oct/Jan). Deposit timing has minor impact on first quarter's interest.
