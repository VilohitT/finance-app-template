## 1. Current interest rate

- **7.1% per annum** for FY 2025-26 (all four quarters confirmed at 7.1% — Q1 through Q4, including Jan-Mar 2026)
- Compounded annually
- Calculated **monthly** on the **lowest balance between the 5th and last day of the month**
- Credited to account on **31 March** each year

Rate is set quarterly by the Ministry of Finance based on the Shyamala Gopinath Committee formula (10-year G-Sec yield + 25 bps spread). However, the government has held the rate at 7.1% since April 2020 despite the formula suggesting lower in some quarters — political/social objective of stable retirement saver returns.

**As-of:** Q4 FY 2025-26 (Jan-Mar 2026) — confirmed 7.1% per Ministry of Finance announcement dated 31 December 2025.

## 2. Eligibility

- Resident Indian individuals (any age — minors via guardian)
- One PPF account per individual (additional accounts get only principal back, no interest)
- Joint accounts: **not allowed**
- HUFs cannot open new PPF accounts (rule changed in 2005; existing HUF accounts grandfathered)
- NRIs cannot open new PPF accounts. NRIs who held an account before becoming non-resident can continue to contribute until original 15-year maturity but cannot extend.
- A parent/guardian can open a PPF account in the name of a minor; that account is independent of the guardian's own.

**For this household:** both user (resident, 24) and father (resident, 58) are eligible. Two separate accounts → ₹3L combined annual capacity. Mother could also have a separate account; check with user during portfolio.md construction whether worth opening.

---

## 3. Contribution

- **Minimum:** ₹500 per financial year (account becomes inactive if not met; reactivation requires ₹50 penalty + minimum deposit)
- **Maximum:** ₹1,50,000 per financial year per account
- **Frequency:** any combination — lump sum, monthly, up to 12 deposits per year
- **Optimal timing for interest accrual:** deposit before the **5th of the month** to earn interest for that full month. For lump-sum annual deposit, depositing on or before **5 April** earns 12 months of interest in that financial year.
- The ₹1.5L cap is **per individual**, not per account. So a person cannot deposit ₹1.5L into their own account and another ₹1.5L into a minor child's account they're the guardian of — combined cannot exceed ₹1.5L.

---

## 4. Lock-in and tenure

- **Initial tenure: 15 years** from the end of the financial year in which the account was opened. Account opened in October 2026 matures on 1 April 2042 (15 years from end of FY 2026-27).
- **Extension:** in 5-year blocks, indefinitely. Can extend with or without further contributions.
- **With contribution extension:** continues to allow ₹1.5L annual deposits, subject to same 80C / EEE rules.
- **Without contribution extension:** existing balance continues to earn interest tax-free; cannot deposit more.

## 5. Premature withdrawal and partial withdrawal rules

### 5.1 Loan against PPF
- Available between **3rd and 6th year** from account opening
- Maximum: **25% of balance at the end of the second preceding year**
- Interest: **1% above the prevailing PPF rate** (currently 8.1% effective) if repaid within 36 months
- If not repaid in 36 months: 6% above PPF rate (13.1% currently) — punitive
- Second loan only after first is fully repaid

### 5.2 Partial withdrawal
- Available from **7th financial year** onward (after completing 5 full FYs)
- Maximum: **50% of the balance at the end of the 4th preceding FY OR balance at end of preceding FY, whichever is lower**
- One withdrawal per financial year
- Withdrawals are tax-free

### 5.3 Premature closure
- Allowed after 5 full FYs only for:
  - Serious illness of self / spouse / dependent children
  - Higher education of self or dependent children
  - Resident becoming non-resident
- Penalty: 1% lower interest applied retrospectively for the entire period

## 6. Tax treatment

PPF is a triple-exempt (EEE) instrument:
- **E** at deposit: contribution eligible for **Section 80C deduction up to ₹1.5L** under Old Regime only
- **E** during accrual: interest is exempt from tax
- **E** at withdrawal: maturity proceeds and partial withdrawals are tax-exempt

### 6.1 Under Old Regime
- ₹1.5L contribution counts toward overall 80C ceiling (alongside EPF/VPF, ELSS, life insurance principal, NSC, SCSS principal, home loan principal, tuition fees)
- Interest tax-free
- Maturity tax-free

### 6.2 Under New Regime
- **No 80C deduction available** at deposit (this is the major change from Budget 2020 onward)
- Interest still tax-free during accrual
- Maturity still tax-free
- **Net effect under new regime:** PPF behaves like a 7.1% fully tax-free EEE wrapper without the deposit-side deduction. Still highly attractive vs taxable debt at 30% bracket — post-tax equivalent of ~10.3% taxable yield.

### 6.3 Comparison to debt MFs in 30% bracket (from `debt-mf.md` Section 8)
- Liquid fund 7.5% gross, 30% slab → 5.16% net
- PPF 7.1% gross, EEE → 7.1% net
- **PPF wins by ~190 bps** for long-term debt allocation in 30% bracket, even before factoring in any 80C deduction.

## 7. Operational notes

- Can be opened at any nationalised bank, major private banks (HDFC, ICICI, Axis), or post offices
- Aadhaar-based eKYC opening available since 2020s (paperless)
- Online deposit / withdrawal supported by all major banks
- Transfer of account between branches / banks is allowed and free
- Nomination: can nominate up to 4 individuals (cumulative); can be modified during account life
- On death of holder: nominee gets balance with accrued interest; account is closed
- Loan against PPF for funding goals: principle compatible (the loan rate is reasonable if repaid in 36 months) but generally not recommended for this household given equity SIPs serve goals more efficiently

For each FY, track per account:
- Deposit YTD
- Remaining capacity for the year (₹1,50,000 – YTD)
- Deposit timing within the month (target: by 5th)
- Cumulative balance
- Maturity date of current 15-year block

## 9. Common errors to avoid

- **Don't open multiple PPF accounts:** subsequent accounts get only principal back, no interest. The ₹1.5L cap is per person, total.
- **Don't miss the 5th-of-month rule:** depositing on the 6th forfeits one month of interest.
- **Don't treat partial withdrawals as the primary liquidity source:** PPF is for long-term goals; the household has a separate ₹50L FD for emergency, so PPF should be left to compound.
- **Don't extend without contribution if planning to actively contribute:** the choice between "extend with contribution" vs "extend without" is a once-per-block decision; switching is messy.
